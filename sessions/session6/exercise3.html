A Monty Hall simulation

This task is advanced and there will be a few programming constructs such as loops and arrays that we have not covered yet. While you'll need to some exploratory learning, this task will really help cement the principles we have learned so far.

Modelling the Monty Hall Problem

First, ensure you fully understand the game:

Suppose you're on a game show and you are given the choice of three doors.

1) Player picks a door
2) Behind one door is a car; behind the others, goats
3) The car and the goats were placed randomly behind the doors before the show
4) The game show host, Monty Hall, who knows what is behind the doors, now has to open one of the two remaining doors, and the door he opens must have a goat behind it
5) He will ask you to decide whether you want to stay with your first choice or to switch to the last remaining door
6) Should you stick or switch?

The question Should you stick or switch? is the question we are trying to answer using a javaScript simulation.
